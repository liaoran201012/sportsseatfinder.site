Ticketmaster sells official primary market tickets directly from venues and teams — you're buying at face value with guaranteed authenticity. StubHub is a resale marketplace where individual sellers list their tickets, which means prices can go up or down based on demand. Both platforms offer buyer guarantees. For sold-out events, StubHub is often your only option.
Yes. Both StubHub and Ticketmaster are industry leaders with strong buyer protections. StubHub's FanProtect Guarantee and Ticketmaster's Verified Tickets program both ensure that if a ticket is invalid at the gate, you'll receive a comparable replacement or a full refund.
Absolutely. StubHub is especially strong for last-minute tickets, with sellers listing seats right up until game time — deliverable instantly as mobile barcodes. Ticketmaster also has last-minute availability for primary market tickets on less popular games.
Both platforms charge service fees on top of the ticket price, typically ranging from 15–30% of the ticket value. Ticketmaster shows fees at checkout before you pay. StubHub introduced a transparent pricing model showing the total cost upfront. It's worth checking both for the same event to compare total prices.
If an event is officially canceled and not rescheduled, both platforms will issue full refunds. StubHub's FanProtect Guarantee covers cancellations, postponements, and venue changes. Ticketmaster's refund policy also covers canceled events directly.
Both platforms primarily deliver tickets as mobile barcodes through their apps or via email — no printing required. You simply show your phone screen at the stadium entrance. Some premium or special tickets may still be delivered physically, but digital delivery is now the standard for all major sports leagues.
