Buying Guide

The Best Time to Buy Sports Tickets (by Sport)

Every sport follows a different pricing pattern. Know exactly when prices peak, when they drop, and which platform to use at each stage.

By SportsSeatFinder Editorial June 2026 5 min read

Ticket prices for live sports are not fixed — they're a market. Prices rise and fall based on demand, proximity to game day, team performance, and even the weather. Understanding when that market tilts in your favor is the single most effective way to save money on live sports.

Here's a sport-by-sport breakdown of the optimal buying windows.

The Universal Rule

For high-demand games (playoffs, rivalry matchups, star appearances): buy immediately when Ticketmaster releases them at face value. For everything else, the cheapest window is usually 2–4 hours before game time on StubHub, when sellers would rather take less than go empty-handed.

NFL Timing Guide

🏈 NFL — 17 Regular Season Games
Months Before
Moderate Ticketmaster face value available. Good for high-demand matchups before they sell out.
2–4 Weeks Before
Most Expensive StubHub resale prices typically peak here. Avoid unless it's a must-see game.
1 Week Before
Moderate Prices start to soften on non-sold-out games as sellers get nervous.
Day of Game
Best Value StubHub prices drop sharply 2–4 hours before kickoff. Aim for this window for regular season games.
Playoffs / Division Games
Buy Early High demand keeps last-minute prices elevated. Use Ticketmaster presale or buy on-sale day.

NBA Timing Guide

🏀 NBA — 82 Regular Season Games
Season Opener / Christmas
Peak Prices High-profile games sell out fast. Buy on Ticketmaster on-sale day or accept high resale premiums.
Mid-Season Weeknight
Best Value Tuesday–Thursday games in January/February offer the lowest prices of the year on StubHub.
Back-to-Back Games
Discount Alert If stars are resting during a back-to-back, prices drop 20–40% last-minute on StubHub.
2 Hours Before Tip-off
Sweet Spot Best last-minute pricing window on StubHub for non-rivalry, non-playoff games.
Playoff Games
Buy at On-Sale First-round tickets are most affordable — grab them from Ticketmaster when first released.

MLB Timing Guide

⚾ MLB — 162 Regular Season Games
Opening Day
Peak Prices Opening Day is the single most expensive regular-season game. Skip it unless you're a superfan.
April–May Weeknights
Cheapest Window Cold weather + empty schedule = rock-bottom prices. Best month of the year for bargains on StubHub.
Same Day (Game Day)
Excellent Value MLB has the best game-day deals in all of sports. Check StubHub 1–2 hours before first pitch.
Rivalry Games
High Demand Yankees–Red Sox, Cubs–Cardinals, and divisional rivalries carry significant premiums. Buy early.
September (Pennant Race)
Variable Prices rise for contending teams late in the season. Non-playoff teams may still offer great deals.

NHL Timing Guide

🏒 NHL — 82 Regular Season Games
Regular Season (Any Day)
Strong Value NHL is consistently the most affordable of the four major leagues for most markets. Face-value often available on Ticketmaster.
Non-Playoff Markets
Best Deal in Sports Rebuilding teams in smaller markets offer incredible deals. Amazing live experience at a fraction of NFL prices.
Outdoor Games / Stadium Series
High Premium Outdoor classic games sell out fast. Use Ticketmaster on-sale day for face value.
Stanley Cup Playoffs
Buy Round 1 Early First-round tickets are still affordable. Prices spike dramatically by Conference Finals.
